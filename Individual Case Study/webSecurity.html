Web Security

What Does Your Browser Know About You?

The percentage of users on the web grows each year. That leads to more and more data becoming accessible to people with potentially malicious intent. One may say to themselves...."I don't use social media or buy anything online my information and identity is secret". This thought although limiting what information is accessed by the web does not completely eliminate the potential that some of your information has been accessed. So what exactly does the browser know about you the user?

The Navigator Object

Let's run some commands on our site to see what these properties can show us. I'll be working with Google Chrome but feel free to use your favorite browser, note that some instructions may not be the same in other browsers:

  1. Right click anywhere on your screen
  2. Click Inspect
  3. Click Console, located as the second option in the toolbar
  4. Let's type in the following commands together
    • console.log("Your location: " + navigator.onLine);
    • console.log("Web browser version: " + navigator.appVersion);
    • console.log("Operating platform: " + navigator.platform);
    • console.log("User agent: " + navigator.userAgent);

The Screen Object

Let's run some more commands on our site to see what these properties can show us. I'll be working with Google Chrome but feel free to use your favorite browser, note that some instructions may not be the same in other browsers:

  1. Right click anywhere on your screen
  2. Click Inspect
  3. Click Console, located as the second option in the toolbar
  4. Let's type in the following commands together
    • console.log("Your current display screen height: " + screen.availHeight);
    • console.log("Your current display screen width: " + screen.availWidth);
    • console.log("The depth of color palette being used: " + screen.colorDepth);
    • console.log("Your screen resolution per pixel is: " + screen.pixelDepth);
Are you suprised by the information accessed by the browser?
